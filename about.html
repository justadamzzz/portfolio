Academic Journey

From Science Stream to Software Development

My academic journey shows how adaptation, discipline, and consistent effort helped me grow from a science-focused background into a committed Computer Science student.

Growth Through Consistency

I did not come from a Computer Science background before entering my degree. My earlier academic path was more focused on science subjects. Because of that, when I first started my Computer Science degree, I had to adapt to many new things such as programming, software development, database systems, and technical problem-solving.

During my first semester in degree, I achieved a GPA of 3.54. At that time, I felt disappointed because I knew I could do better. However, instead of giving up, that result became my motivation to improve myself. I started to study harder, manage my time better, practice more coding, and slowly build confidence in Computer Science.

Because of that effort, my results improved in the following semesters. I achieved 3.90 in Semester 2, 4.00 in Semester 3, 3.92 in Semester 4, and 3.83 in Semester 5. Now, I am in Semester 6 with a current CGPA of 3.84. This journey shows my ability to adapt, improve, and stay committed even when starting from a different academic background.
